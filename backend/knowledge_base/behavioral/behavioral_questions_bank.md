# Behavioral Question Bank — Model Answers and Rubrics

**Type:** Behavioral interview preparation
**Use:** Grounding for scoring and coaching candidate answers to common behavioral prompts.

## Q1: "Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a coworker."

**What interviewers look for:** emotional maturity, willingness to understand others, outcome focus.
**Model answer (STAR):** Disagreement on architecture → built a data-driven comparison → ran a short spike → aligned on the safer option → shipped, reduced incidents, preserved the relationship.
**Red flags:** blaming, "winning", no resolution.

## Q2: "Describe a time you failed."

**What interviewers look for:** ownership, root-cause thinking, durable fixes.
**Model answer:** Real failure with consequences → owned your part → identified root cause → implemented a systemic change → measurable improvement after.
**Red flags:** fake weakness, blaming circumstances, no learning.

## Q3: "Tell me about a time you led without authority."

**What interviewers look for:** initiative, influence, accountability.
**Model answer:** Spotted an unowned problem → rallied stakeholders with a clear plan → drove it to completion → quantified impact.

## Q4: "How do you handle competing priorities / tight deadlines?"

**What interviewers look for:** prioritization frameworks, communication, scope negotiation.
**Model answer:** Clarify the goal and constraints → rank by impact/effort → negotiate scope with stakeholders early → deliver the critical path → communicate trade-offs transparently.

## Q5: "Tell me about a time you received difficult feedback."

**What interviewers look for:** coachability, self-awareness, follow-through.
**Model answer:** Received specific critique → resisted defensiveness → made a concrete behavior change → showed measurable improvement and follow-up.

## Q6: "Describe a time you disagreed with your manager."

**What interviewers look for:** respectful candor, data over ego, commitment after the decision.
**Model answer:** Voiced a reasoned, data-backed concern privately → manager decided → you disagreed-and-committed → reflected on the outcome.

## Q7: "Tell me about a project you're proud of."

**What interviewers look for:** scope of ownership, technical/product judgment, impact.
**Model answer:** Why it mattered → your specific contribution → key decisions and trade-offs → measurable result → what you'd do differently.

## Q8: "How do you handle ambiguity?"

**What interviewers look for:** structured problem-solving, bias to action, comfort making reversible decisions.
**Model answer:** Break the problem down → identify the riskiest assumption → run a cheap test → decide and iterate.

## Q9: "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond."

**What interviewers look for:** ownership beyond the job description, customer/team empathy.

## Q10: "Describe a time you had to learn something quickly."

**What interviewers look for:** learning strategy, resourcefulness, applying knowledge under time pressure.

## Scoring Rubric (0–100)

- **80–100:** Clear STAR structure, explicit personal ownership, quantified result, reflective learning.
- **55–79:** Relevant story, some structure, partial metrics, mostly "I" not "we".
- **30–54:** Vague, "we"-heavy, no measurable outcome, weak relevance.
- **0–29:** Off-topic, no real example, or only generic statements.
